# About Ngoc Cao

I'm Ngoc Cao, Chief Design Officer at **QUICKOM** — an event infrastructure-as-a-service platform that integrates registration, ticketing, livestreaming, AI-assisted engagement, real-time translation, and analytics into one system. The platform supports events with up to **100,000 concurrent online attendees** and has served **hundreds of thousands of users across thousands of events** in Vietnam and surrounding markets.

I joined QUICKOM in 2020 and have been on the executive team since, building the design function from the ground up and shaping product, brand, and growth strategy alongside engineering, product, and business leadership.

## What I'm working on now

Most of my current focus is on **QUICKOM for Education**, a new vertical we're building on top of the core platform. The idea is straightforward: universities run on the same coordination problem that events do — classrooms, conferences, multi-campus programs, faculty IP — but the tools are fragmented across LMS, video conferencing, registration, and analytics systems that don't talk to each other. We're positioning QUICKOM as an *operating system for the digital university*, integrating hybrid classrooms, LMS workflows (Canvas, Moodle, or our native LMS), AI learning assistance, faculty IP protection, and institutional dashboards.

The vertical is already in use with institutional partners including **RMIT Vietnam, UEH, Foreign Trade University, and FPT University**, and it's quietly become the most exciting design challenge I've worked on — not because the surface is bigger, but because the design decisions ripple into how thousands of students and faculty actually learn and teach.

## How I got here

My background is in graphic design (BA, University of Architecture Ho Chi Minh City, 2019), but my career has steadily pulled me from craft into systems. At **Beowulf Blockchain** (2018–2020), I owned UI/UX and art direction across a blockchain product portfolio — my first real exposure to designing for technical complexity. At QUICKOM, that scope expanded again: brand, product, team, strategy, and the difficult business of translating creative vision into something engineering can actually ship.

Working at the intersection of design and business kept exposing the same gap in my own training. I could lead a design org, but I wanted to lead a *company* — and I needed the financial, strategic, and leadership vocabulary to do it well. That's why I'm pursuing my **MBA at VEMBA (University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa)**, with the intention of continuing to a DBA afterward.

## Where I'm headed

The long-term goal sits at the intersection of three things I care about: **art, business, and technology**. Concretely, that probably means one of two paths:

- **Leading as a CEO** of a design-driven technology company, where creative direction and business strategy aren't separate functions reporting to each other but a single integrated discipline; or
- **Teaching** — helping the next generation of creative leaders develop the business and strategic foundations that most design education still leaves out.

I suspect I'll do some version of both. The MBA → DBA → academia route makes the second path concrete; the first is what I'm already doing every day at QUICKOM.
